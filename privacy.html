Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 5, 2026

Short version: Nimva stores your schedule data on your device and in your private iCloud account. We have no backend server, collect no analytics, and never see your data. Your information is yours.

What this policy covers

This policy describes how Nimva (the iOS app) handles your information. It applies to all versions of the Nimva app available on the App Store.

Information we do not collect

Nimva does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on our servers — because we don't have any servers. Specifically:

  • No account creation. There are no Nimva accounts.
  • No analytics or usage tracking. We do not use any third-party analytics, crash reporting, or telemetry SDKs.
  • No advertising identifiers. We do not access your IDFA or any advertising ID.
  • No location data. Nimva never requests or uses your location.
  • No contacts or calendar data unless you explicitly use the optional calendar import feature — and even then, data stays on your device.

Data stored on your device and in iCloud

All data you enter into Nimva — your events, energy labels, schedule history, and check-in responses — is stored in two places:

  • Your device via SwiftData (Apple's local database framework).
  • Your private iCloud account via CloudKit, Apple's sync service. This data lives in a private database that only you can access. Apple stores it on their servers as part of your iCloud account — Nimva cannot read it, and neither can anyone else.

If you delete the Nimva app, all local data is deleted. You can also delete your iCloud data by going to Settings → [your name] → iCloud → Manage Account Storage on your iPhone.

Calendar import (optional)

Nimva includes an optional feature to import events from Apple Calendar. If you use this feature:

  • Nimva requests read access to your calendars via Apple's EventKit framework.
  • Imported events are stored locally alongside your other Nimva data — they are never sent anywhere outside your device or iCloud.
  • You can revoke calendar access at any time in Settings → Privacy & Security → Calendars on your iPhone.

Subscriptions and payments

Nimva PRO is an optional subscription managed entirely by Apple through StoreKit 2. Nimva never sees, stores, or processes your payment information. All billing is handled by Apple and subject to Apple's Terms of Service.

Notifications

Nimva may send local notifications (for example, reminders about your schedule). These are scheduled on your device using Apple's notification framework — no notification server is involved, and no data leaves your device to send them.

Children's privacy

Nimva is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children. If you believe a child has provided information through the app, please contact us and we will address it promptly.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes meaningfully, we will update the "last updated" date above. Continued use of the app after a policy update constitutes acceptance of the new policy.
